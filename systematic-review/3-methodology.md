# 2. Methodology

This systematic review was conducted in accordance with the Preferred Reporting Items for Systematic Reviews and Meta-Analyses (PRISMA) guidelines to ensure a rigorous, objective, and reproducible approach to identifying and selecting relevant literature.

## 2.1. Search Strategy
A comprehensive literature search was performed using major scientific electronic databases (e.g., Scopus, Web of Science, and ArXiv) to identify relevant studies. The search aimed to capture the intersection of space weather monitoring and artificial intelligence, specifically focusing on ionospheric irregularities. Due to limitations in search string complexity and inconsistent retrieval of relevant results, the IEEE Xplore database was not included in the final analysis.

## 2.2. Search String
The search string was constructed using Boolean operators to combine keywords related to the target atmospheric phenomena and the computational methodologies. The conceptual search string combined terms such as ("Equatorial Plasma Bubble" OR "EPB" OR "ionospheric irregularities" OR "traveling ionospheric disturbances") AND ("Machine Learning" OR "Deep Learning" OR "Artificial Intelligence" OR "Neural Network" OR "Computer Vision"). The syntax was adapted to fit the specific formatting requirements of each database.

## 2.3. Inclusion Criteria
To determine the eligibility of the retrieved articles, the following inclusion criteria were established:
1. Peer-reviewed, original research articles.
2. Studies explicitly applying Machine Learning or Deep Learning algorithms.
3. Studies focused on the automated detection, classification, segmentation, or prediction of Equatorial Plasma Bubbles (or closely related ionospheric structures like MSTIDs).
4. Studies utilizing relevant observational data (e.g., All-Sky Imager optical data, GNSS-TEC, or VHF radar).
5. Articles published in the English language.

## 2.4. Exclusion Criteria
Articles were excluded from the review if they met any of the following criteria:
1. Review papers, conference abstracts without full text, book chapters, or editorials.
2. Studies analyzing Equatorial Plasma Bubbles using solely traditional manual or statistical methods without Machine Learning components.
3. Studies applying Machine Learning to space weather phenomena unrelated to ionospheric plasma bubbles or disturbances.
4. Studies where the full text was inaccessible.

## 2.5. Duplicate Removal and Data Management
Following the execution of the search strategy across the selected databases, all retrieved citations were exported to **Zotero**, a reference management software. Zotero was utilized to consolidate the records and automatically identify and remove duplicate publications, generating a list of unique records for the screening phase.

## 2.6. Study Selection Process (Screening)
The screening process was conducted manually in two distinct stages to ensure a thorough evaluation of the literature:
*   **Stage 1 (Title and Abstract Screening):** The titles and abstracts of all unique records were manually screened by the researcher to assess their initial relevance to the research topic. Articles clearly falling outside the scope of the review were excluded at this stage.
*   **Stage 2 (Full-Text Screening):** The articles that passed the initial screening were retrieved in full text and read in their entirety by the researcher. A comprehensive manual evaluation of each manuscript was conducted against the predefined inclusion and exclusion criteria to confirm methodological relevance and definitively decide what to include or exclude for data extraction.
