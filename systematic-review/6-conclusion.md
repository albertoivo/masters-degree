# 5. Conclusion

This systematic review demonstrates that the integration of machine learning into space weather monitoring represents a significant methodological advancement for the automated detection, classification, and feature extraction of Equatorial Plasma Bubbles (EPBs). By synthesizing recent literature, this review highlights that deep convolutional neural networks and hybrid machine learning architectures can effectively identify complex EPB morphologies from optical imagery and radar data, achieving high precision while drastically reducing processing time. Consequently, this study contributes a consolidated overview of the state-of-the-art computational frameworks, serving as a baseline for transitioning from subjective manual observation to automated, real-time operational systems. However, the synthesized literature reveals persistent limitations that constrain current model robustness. Chief among these are the severe performance degradation of optical-based models under adverse tropospheric weather conditions—such as dense cloud cover and artificial light pollution—and model biases stemming from imbalanced training datasets heavily skewed by the solar cycle. 

Despite the high accuracies reported for reactive detection and segmentation tasks, a critical research gap remains in the development of proactive models capable of forecasting the initiation and subsequent spatiotemporal evolution of EPBs. Furthermore, existing automated frameworks predominantly rely on isolated, single-instrument data sources, which limits their continuous operational reliability. Future research should prioritize the formulation of multimodal data fusion architectures that concurrently ingest optical, radar, and GNSS-TEC measurements to mitigate the environmental constraints inherent to individual sensors. Additionally, advancing toward sequence-based deep learning models could facilitate predictive spatiotemporal forecasting, while the continued integration of explainable artificial intelligence will be vital for ensuring model transparency and driving new physical insights into ionospheric dynamics.
