# Abstract

Equatorial Plasma Bubbles (EPBs) cause severe ionospheric scintillations, disrupting global satellite communication and navigation systems. While continuous monitoring is critical, traditional manual detection in observational data is subjective and unscalable. This systematic literature review investigates the integration of Machine Learning (ML) techniques to automate the detection, classification, and feature extraction of EPBs. Following the PRISMA protocol, a comprehensive search was conducted across Scopus, Web of Science, and ArXiv, identifying 11 relevant studies published between 2022 and 2025. The synthesis reveals a dominant reliance on All-Sky Imager (ASI) optical data and Very High Frequency (VHF) radar imagery. Deep Convolutional Neural Networks (e.g., ResNet, Mask R-CNN) and hybrid architectures (e.g., CNN-SVM, 2DPCA-Random Forest) constitute the primary methodological trends. These models consistently achieve detection accuracies exceeding 90%, with optimized ensemble and Explainable AI (XAI) frameworks reaching over 99% while drastically reducing computational costs. Furthermore, automated morphological segmentation has enabled the first solar-cycle-scale statistical profiling of EPB drift and latitudinal extension. ML represents a transformative paradigm in space weather monitoring, offering robust, real-time operational capabilities. Future research must address prevailing limitations, specifically dataset imbalances and susceptibility to tropospheric cloud contamination, by advancing multi-instrument data fusion and predictive spatiotemporal modeling.
